Type 2 Diabetes is a chronic disorder resulting from complex insulin production/secretion that has long-lasting impacts on the individual's general health and overall well-being. While 462 million people, or 6.26 % of the world's population, are impacted by the problem, an even more worrying factor is its correlation with increased BMI or obesity. Obesity or an overall BMI of 30 kg/m or more is the result or alternatively results in excess accumulation of adipose tissue, which has been observed to trigger excess inflammation that, in turn, impacts the insulin metabolism of the body.

Animal studies have shown excess calories or saturated fats to disrupt hypothalamic insulin-signaling and lipid-sensing mechanisms, resulting in impaired beta cell production with a subsequent transition from pre-diabetes to Type-2 Diabetes. Alternatively, insufficient insulin metabolism as a result of diabetes results in the body storing glucose in the tissues as fat and an impact on the overall BMI. The American Diabetes Association has stated that being overweight can increase the predisposition to diabetes and make you prone to heart problems and potential heart stroke. It can even increase the risk of cholesterol and impact your blood pressure.

What Is RYBELSUS

Rybelsus is an FDA-approved oral Semaglutide that has shown impressive results in controlling Type-2 diabetes. It was approved in 2019 in the United States and in Japan and other Asian countries in 2020 for the management of Type-2 Diabetes and, in a short period, has received a favourable response as an effective antidiabetic.

Clinical trials have shown impressive outcomes for Rybelsus in managing A1C levels ( a test to measure the average level of glucose in the blood over the past three months ) compared to medications like Jardiance, a derivative of Empagliflozin and a potent antidiabetic.

Evaluation of oral Semaglutide, the active ingredient of Rybelsus, in ten trials involving over eight thousand patients with type-2 diabetes, has shown the medication to improve the HbA1c levels ( average glucose/blood sugar levels for the last two to three months) by 0.6-0.9% at a lower dose of 3 mg daily, result in a 0.8-1.2% improvement at a dose of 7 mg and cause 1.0-1.4% improvement at a higher dose of 14 mg. The medication showed better results than placebo and other antidiabetics used comparatively.

The average HbA1c was approximately 8%, and improvements were marked after four to eight weeks. 14 mg of the medication resulted in a < 7.0% HbA1c compared to other medicines used in the study.

RYBELSUS® May Help You Lose Some Weight.

Although Rybelsus is not a weight-loss drug, Type-2 Diabetics have reported an impressive change in BMI with its use. In the above-mentioned study, patients using oral Semaglutide/ Rybelsus observed an impressive reduction in body weight after twenty-six weeks, other than a positive change in the body's glycaemic parameters. 3 mg of oral Semaglutide resulted in a 1.2-1.5kg weight loss, 7 mg of oral Semaglutide caused a 2.2-2.4 kg weight loss, and 14 mg of oral Semaglutide caused a 3.1-4.4 kg weight loss at the end of twenty-six weeks. The trials further proved that Rybelsus halved the risk of death in comparison with placebo when used in individuals with cardiovascular disease.

The study showed that oral Semaglutide, other than improving the glycaemic index of the system, can prove to be an effective weight loss medication when used along with diet and exercise. And it is equally effective on individuals of all ethnicities.

In another study conducted to evaluate the efficacy of Rybesis in comparison to Empagliflozin in Type-2 Diabetics with increased BMI not responding to Metformin, a randomized, open-label, multinational fifty-two-week trial was conducted at one hundred and eight sites in 12 countries including Argentina, Russia, Spain, Croatia, Greece Hungary, Italy, Poland, and Brazil. A total of 822 subjects were finalized after the initial screening. Patients were randomized to either Rybelsus 14 mg or Empagliflozin 25 mg for twenty-five weeks, added by a follow-up of five weeks.

Rybelsus was administered as a starting dose of 3 mg once daily for four weeks, and then the strength increased to 7 mg and 14 mg in the next two weeks. Because of its oral nature, patients were instructed to have the tablet on an empty stomach with at least 120 ml of water thirty minutes before breakfast or before using any other oral medication. The comparative medication, Empagliflozin, was instructed to be used at a starting strength of 10 mg once daily for eight weeks, after which it was provided at an increased strength of 25 mg.

While changes in HbA or glucose levels were observed, and patients reported a controlled blood glucose level after Rybelsus use, they also reported a weight loss of more than 5% in lower doses and more than 10% in higher strengths of the medication without impacting the glycaemic parameters in an adverse manner. The participants started observing weight changes from the fourth week and reported a decrease in appetite with a feeling of fullness that helped.

Important Safety Information

Rybelsus use can trigger thyroid tumors in certain conditions. Randomized controlled trials and a meta-analysis of medical literature have pointed out that the risk of thyroid cancer, pancreatic cancer, and all neoplasms is not prevalent in Semaglutide users. Use of Rybelsus can result in nausea, vomiting, and gastrointestinal issues. SUSTAIN 6 studies have shown a higher incidence of gastrointestinal disturbances in older people using the medicine. In the trials, the side effects were recorded to be higher in oral Semaglutide ( Rybelsus ) than subcutaneous Semaglutide, resulting in 27% of the subjects discontinuing oral Semaglutide and 14% of users discontinuing subcutaneous Semaglutide.

Precaution

Studies with animals have shown reproductive toxicity while using oral Semaglutide, the chief ingredient of Rybelsus. The European Medicine Agency advises females planning pregnancy to discontinue the medicine before planning for pregnancy. At times, patients treated with Rybelsus while using insulin or Sulfonylurea can increase the risk of hypoglycemia on using the two medicines concomitantly. For safety reasons, you should discuss with the physician about reducing the dose of either medicine when using them concomitantly. SUSTAIN 6 trials have shown a higher risk of Diabetic retinopathy. A meta-analysis of twenty-three randomized trials involving 22,096 patients has shown that Semaglutide use is associated with an increased risk of Diabetic retinopathy, especially in older people above the age of sixty. While semaglutide use is not directly related to Diabetic retinopathy, medical practitioners advise caution in older patients using the drug or in people with a history of Diabetic Retinopathy.

How should I take RYBELSUS®?

Some of the most important things to remember when you are taking Rybelsus are

  • Use the medication as per the dose recommended by your healthcare provider.
  • Do not crush or chew the pill. Swallow it whole with sufficient water.
  • Keep an interval of at least thirty minutes between the use of Rybelsus and other medications.
  • If you miss a dose of Rybelsus, use it immediately if it is less than 6 hours. If not, skip the dose and use it in the subsequent usage schedule period.

What are the possible side effects of RYBELSUS®?

Rybelsus use can result in side effects like.

  • Pancreatitis
  • Gastrointestinal side effects.
  • Nausea
  • Inflammation of the gallbladder
  • Allergic reactions if you are hypersensitive to the ingredients of the drug

Do not use the medicine if you develop side effects or adverse reactions to using the drug. Nausea and vomiting can result in dehydration, and you should drink sufficient water during this period. If you experience extreme symptoms, discontinue Rybelsus use and get urgent consultation.
