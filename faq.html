Ryebelsus FAQ's

1. Why do I need to be hungry before taking RYBELSUS? What will happen if I eat or drink it?

You should take RYBELSUS first thing in the morning without food because it can change how the medicine is taken and make it less effective. The amount of RYBELSUS that gets into your system can be greatly reduced if you take it with food. Taking RYBELSUS at least 30 minutes before your first meal, drink, or oral medicine of the day will help it work better.

2. What are the most common or dangerous side effects of RYBELSUS that I need to be aware of?

Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, loss of appetite, bloating, and constipation are the most common side effects of RYBELSUS. Some of the more dangerous side effects to watch out for are pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas), gallbladder issues, hypoglycemia (low blood sugar), kidney issues, and allergic reactions. If you have serious side effects, you should call your doctor right away.

3. How long does it take for RYBELSUS to begin working? When will I notice that my blood sugar has changed?

RYBELSUS may take up to two weeks to fully lower your blood sugar. In the first one to two weeks of treatment, you should see a change in your fasting blood glucose levels. When you start taking RYBELSUS, your doctor will keep a close eye on your blood sugar and may change your dose after seeing how it works.

4. Can I take other drugs or vitamins along with RYBELSUS? Should I be worried about any interactions?

You should tell your doctor about all of the medicines, vitamins, and supplements you are taking because some of them may not work well with RYBELSUS. Opioids, antidepressants, and aluminum/magnesium antacids are some examples of drugs that can make it harder for the stomach to empty. These drugs should not be taken for at least 30 minutes after taking RYBELSUS.

5. How long do I need to take RYBELSUS? Should I take this medicine for a short time or the rest of my life?

RYBELSUS is meant to be used for a long time to treat type 2 diabetes. You may have to keep taking it indefinitely to help control your blood sugar unless your doctor says it's no longer helping or is giving you bad side effects. You should talk to your doctor before stopping RYBELSUS.

6. What should I do if I forget to take my RYBELSUS dose? Should I skip the dose I missed or take two pills the next day?

If you forget to take a dose, remember to take it as planned the next morning. Do not take an extra tablet or two tablets in one day to make up for a dose that you forgot. If you take more than the dosage prescribed by the doctor, you may be more likely to have side effects.

7. If I already have heart disease or kidney trouble, is it safe for me to use RYBELSUS?

RYBELSUS hasn't been studied in much detail in people who have major kidney problems or diabetic retinopathy. Tell your doctor about all of your health problems so they can decide if RYBELSUS is safe and right for you to use. If you have certain conditions, your doctor may need to change the dose or keep a closer eye on you.

8. What are the risks of taking RYBELSUS while nursing or while you are pregnant? If I get pregnant, should I stop taking it?

Pregnant women have not been studied with RYBELSUS, so we do not know what risks they may face. If you are pregnant, you shouldn't take RYBELSUS. If you get pregnant while taking this medicine, you should tell your doctor right away. Also, no one knows if RYBELSUS gets into breast milk. Talk to your doctor about the pros and cons of using RYBELSUS while you are nursing.

9. What is the price of RYBELSUS? Does your insurance cover it? Are there any programs that can help patients?

Without insurance, a month's supply of RYBELSUS can cost anywhere from $850 to $1,000. Many insurance companies pay for some or all of RYBELSUS. The RYBELSUS Savings Card can help pay your copays up to $500 a month. Novo Nordisk also has programs to help patients who don't have insurance or don't have enough insurance. To find out more, talk to your doctor or go to RYBELSUS.com.

10. What kind of doctor should give me RYBELSUS and keep an eye on it? Should I see an endocrinologist, or can my general care doctor do this?

RYBELSUS should only be prescribed and checked on by a doctor who knows how to treat diabetes, like a general care doctor or an endocrinologist. If your primary care doctor knows diabetes drugs, they can write you a prescription for RYBELSUS. It is always best to keep regular meetings with the people who care for your diabetes.

11. Are Ozempic and Rybelsus the same kind of medicine?

Both Ozempic and Rybelsus are GLP-1 receptor agonists that are used to treat type 2 diabetes, but they are not the same drug. Rybelsus is taken by mouth, while Ozempic is shot. In some ways, they both drop blood sugar in the same way, but their molecular structures are not the same.

12. Is Rybelsus only used to help people lose weight, or is it also used for other things?

Adults with type 2 diabetes are given Rybelsus to help them better control their blood sugar. Losing weight is not the main goal of the medicine; it is a side effect.

13. How does Rybelsus work in the body to make things happen?

Rybelsus acts like a hormone called GLP-1, which the body releases naturally after a meal. GLP-1 slows down processing and makes you feel less hungry. When rybelsus links to receptors in the body, it lowers blood sugar and helps people lose weight.

14. Can people who don't have diabetes take Rybelsus easily and effectively?

Rybelsus can only be used by people with type 2 diabetes, according to the FDA. There isn't enough proof to show that it is safe for people who don't have diabetes.

15. Is there proof that Ozempic or Rybelsus is less likely to cause side effects?

No head-to-head studies have been done to compare the safety features of Ozempic and Rybelsus. Both may cause stomach problems like sickness, vomiting, and diarrhea.

16. How long does it usually take to start losing weight after starting Rybelsus?

Most people who take Rybelsus to lose weight see effects in three to six months. After 12 to 18 months of use, the most weight loss is seen. Making small changes to your habits over time can help you lose weight.

17. What are the right steps to follow when taking a dose of Rybelsus?

You should take Rybelsus once a day in the morning, at least half an hour before you eat or drink anything. The pill is taken all the way down with a sip of water. It shouldn't be chewed, broken, or split.

18. Is it okay to take Rybelsus with other pills that you take by mouth?

No, you shouldn't eat or drink anything before taking Rybelsus. Taking it with food or other drugs can change how well it works and how quickly it is absorbed.

19. Why is it important to not eat or drink before taking Rybelsus?

Rybelsus works best when taken without food or on an empty stomach. Food, especially meals high in fat, can make it much harder for the medicine to work.

20. What might happen if you forget to take your Rybelsus dose or don't follow the directions?

If you miss a dose or take Rybelsus the wrong way, it may not work as well to lower your blood sugar. Remember to take it every day as directed and always follow the directions on how to use it.

21. Can someone who doesn't have diabetes take Rybelsus?

No, Rybelsus is a drug that can only be bought with a prescription and is only used to treat type 2 diabetes. When someone with diabetes is under the care of a doctor, they should only take it.

22. Can someone who doesn't have diabetes take Rybelsus to help them lose weight?

There is no way to lose weight with Rybelsus, and people who don't have diabetes should never take it. It can be dangerous to take prescription drugs without a medical reason.

23. What are the possible bad things that could happen if you take Rybelsus?

Rybelsus can cause side effects like feeling sick, throwing up, diarrhea, losing your appetite, bloating, and constipation. Another thing it might do is give some people thyroid tumors or gastritis. As with any treatment, there is a chance that you will be allergic to Rybelsus.

24. Is Rybelsus generally thought to be a safe drug?

Yes, Rybelsus is usually thought to be safe and effective when taken by people with type 2 diabetes as directed by a doctor. Lack of blood sugar, heart issues, or other major side effects were not more likely to happen in clinical trials.

25. In general, how much does a prescription for Rybelsus cost?

If you don't have insurance, the cash price for 30 days of Rybelsus can be anywhere from $900 to $1,100. When you have insurance, copays can be very different based on the plan. Some programs can lower the costs of being a patient.

26. How does Rybelsus drop blood sugar?

The main ingredient in Rybelsus is semaglutide, which works like a hormone called GLP-1 to help keep blood sugar levels steady. When blood sugar is high, it works by increasing the release of insulin and lowering the release of glucagon. This mix helps bring down A1c.

27. What are some bad impacts that can happen when you take Rybelsus?

Rybelsus can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, loss of hunger, constipation, abdominal pain, headaches, tiredness, dizziness, and reactions at the injection site. Thyroid C-cell tumors and pancreatitis are two of the more major risks.

28. Is Rybelsus okay to use for things other than diabetes?

There isn't. Rybelsus is only approved by the FDA to help people with type 2 diabetes better control their blood sugar, along with diet and exercise. It can't be used for anything else.

29. What is the length of time that Rybelsus has been sold as a diabetes medicine?

The FDA gave the go-ahead for Rybelsus in September 2019. It has been out for about three and a half years as of early 2023. It was the first GLP-1 drug that could be taken by mouth that was approved.
